
The friction coefficient (μ) determines the maximum tire force via:
    F_max = μ * F_z
where F_z is the vertical (normal) load on the tire.

Below are typical values for μ under different conditions:

| Surface Type                   | Typical μ Range |
|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Dry asphalt (performance tires) | 0.9 – 1.2       |
| Dry asphalt (regular tires)     | 0.7 – 1.0       |
| Wet asphalt                     | 0.5 – 0.7       |
| Snow                            | 0.2 – 0.3       |
| Ice                             | 0.05 – 0.1      |
| Racing slick on dry track       | 1.3 – 1.6       |

Recommended μ values for simulation:

- Ferrari / Lamborghini / Bugatti (dry asphalt, high-performance tires): μ = 1.1–1.2
- Tesla Model S Plaid (dry asphalt, street performance tires): μ ≈ 1.05
- Regular sedan (all-season tires on dry asphalt): μ ≈ 0.9
- Wet weather simulation (any car): μ ≈ 0.6
- Snow/ice simulation (any car): μ ≈ 0.2 – 0.05
- Race track with slicks (track-only simulation): μ ≈ 1.4

Note:
Choose μ based on the tire type and road condition you're simulating.
For consistent simulations across cars, ensure that μ is paired with appropriate Pacejka parameters and vertical loads.
